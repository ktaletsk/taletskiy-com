I was at Apple Store waiting for my Genius Bar appointment and saw a class happening there on a big screen: [Swift Playgrounds](https://developer.apple.com/swift-playground/).

![Learn to Code class](/img/apple-class.jpg)

Swift Playground is such a cool and underuappreciated interactive environment! Left side renders REPL


It would be so cool to have this in Python, I thought. I was playing lots with pywidget and anywidget lately and the plan practically wrote itself in my head: reproduce the same delightful experience in Python, with help of anywidget and marimo.

Introducing **Wanderland**!

Wanderland allows to define an interactive 2D world with its lanscape, objects and actions. User then can write functions corresponding to allowed actions to move character in that world and solve the puzzle. I aimed to reprodcue the same delight that Swift Playgrounds brought me and the first thing to build was a character! Since I was building in marimo I remembered that Vincent [created](https://discord.com/channels/1059888774789730424/1179936876103225354/1505901266650529882) an (unofficial) marimo mascot - Mo the Mossball.

With the help of Nano Banana, I made the character sheets - and then Claude did the rest to bring it to life in 3D.
